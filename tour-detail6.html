  • Day 1 Arrival. Ulaanbaatar

    Arrive in Ulaanbaatar, you will be welcomed by Ongolog tour guide and transfer to your centrally located Hotel. After lunch take a city tour to Gandan Monastery where you will see 25 meters tall gold plated statue of Magjid Janraisig. Gandan is the largest and most important monastery of Mongolia, with over 400 monks. Next, we will visit the National History Museum. Exhibition of museum covers history of Mongolia, ethnography and traditional life. Overnight at the hotel. (D)

     Dinner
    Highlights

    Gandan monastery

    Gandan Monastery is a Tibetan-style Buddhist Monastery. Its name means ”the big place of the complete joy”. Nowadays, many hundreds of monks live there. It houses a 26,5-metres (87 feet) high statue of Megjid-Janraiseg. Megjid-Janraiseg is a bodhisattva, a sage who leads the men on the way of Truth. He represents compassion.

    National history museum

    The National History Museum, formerly named “Museum of the Revolution”, opened in 1991. It has important collections of nomadic or sacred art, craft industry, and domestic and technical objects (used for breeding, for example), and is a very good introduction to the history of Mongolia from Prehistory to nowadays. Among the 15.000 museum pieces, we can notably see some carpets and silk objects dating back from the Huns period, and some leather boots belonging to a Mongolian soldier of this period.

    Central square statue of Chinggis Khaans

    In July 1921, in the centre of Ulan-Bator, the ”hero of the revolution” Damdin Sukhbaatar declared the independence of Mongolia from China. Then his name was given to the square, Sukhbaatar Square.

    In the centre of the square, a statue of Sukhbaatar riding his horse still stands. The words he apparently pronounced at the moment of independence are engraved on the bottom of the statue : “If we, the whole people, unite in our common effort and common will, there will be nothing in the world that we cannot achieve, that we will not have learnt or that we will have failed to do”.

  • Day 2 Elsen tasarkhai sand dunes


    Today we will drive to Elsen tasarkhai an area consisting of mixes of all landscapes: mountains, sands and rivers. After lunch we will visit a nomad family, explore their unique lifestyle, and experience horse or camel riding. Overnight in a ger camp. (B+L+D)

  • Day 3 Kharkhorum and Erdenezuu


    In the morning, drive to Kharkhorum, which was the capital of the Mongol Empire between 1235 and 1260, and of the Northern Yuan in the 14–15th centuries and visit Erdene Zuu Buddhist Monastery, established on the ruins of Karakorum in 1586 by AbtaiSain Khan. Overnight in a ger camp. (B+L+D)

     Breakfast   Lunch   Dinner 

  • Day 4 Hustain Nuruu National park


    After breakfast, we will drive to Hustain Nuruu National park which houses Takhi horses (best known as Prejwalski’s horses). Visit Prejwalski’s horses’ museum and watch documentary about Takhi horse. Go hiking, sight see around the National Park, and enjoy the beautiful scenery. Overnight in a ger camp. (B+L+D)

     Breakfast   Lunch   Dinner 

  • Day 5 Ulaanbaatar


    Today we will drive back to Ulaanbaatar city and check into your hotel. In the evening we will watch a live performance of Mongolian National folklore. Shopping for souvenir and cashmere is optional. Overnight at the hotel. (B+L+D)

     Breakfast   Lunch   Dinner 

  • Day 6


    After breakfast, you will be transferred to the airport or train station for international departure.
